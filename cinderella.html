About the character
Once upon a time, in the heart of Disney's magical realm, emerged a heroine named Cinderella, the star of their 1950 animated masterpiece. Tragedy struck early in her life when she lost her mother, and her father, believing she needed a mother's love, remarried. However, fate was cruel, and he too passed away soon after, leaving Cinderella at the mercy of her wicked stepmother, Lady Tremaine, and her two spiteful stepsisters, Anastasia and Drizella. Forced into a life of servitude, Cinderella became a scullery maid. Yet, despite the harshness of her stepfamily, she refused to let it tarnish her spirit. She remained a beacon of kindness, her spirit unbroken, her inner beauty shining brighter than ever. Her unwavering faith and eternal optimism summoned a Fairy Godmother, whose enchanting magic transformed Cinderella's life from a humble servant to a radiant princess. Cinderella, with her tale of resilience and transformation, became the second jewel in the crown of Disney's Princess line-up, following the trail blazed by Snow White. Her story continues to inspire and captivate, a testament to the power of hope and kindness.
